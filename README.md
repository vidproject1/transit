# Transit

## Full Game Concept
The premise. You are a **Sovereign Courier** — one of maybe a dozen people in the city who operate outside every faction's jurisdiction because you're too useful to kill and too dangerous to control. You carry anything, for anyone, anywhere. The city's power brokers need you more than you need them. They just haven't fully accepted that yet.

## The Core Gameplay Loop
Every run follows the same satisfying rhythm:
**Accept a contract → plan your route → execute at speed → survive any ambush → deliver and collect.**

The planning layer is lightweight — you look at the city map, pick a path through 2-3 districts, and mentally clock which factions control the checkpoints. The execution is where it sings. You're moving fast through vertical, dense environments, and anyone who gets in your way gets put down efficiently and without drama. You don't hide. You don't sneak. You walk through the front door and dare them to stop you.

**The twist that keeps it interesting**: Contracts have a Heat rating. High-heat packages attract faction interceptors mid-run — timed ambushes that force you off your planned route into improvised firefights across rooftops, transit tunnels, and crowded market floors. Low-heat contracts are clean money. High-heat contracts pay for upgrades.

## The City — District Physics Rules
Each of the five districts has a distinct physics modifier that changes how movement and combat feel:

| District | Modifier | Faction | Vibe |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Canopy** | Low gravity — long floaty jumps | The Architects | Glass towers, white light, money |
| **The Compress** | 2x time dilation — everything is slower | Chronos Syndicate | Casino district frozen in amber |
| **The Trench** | High gravity — fast falls, brutal impacts | Iron Collective | Industrial, ground-level, brutal |
| **The Veil** | Fog of war — augmented vision required | Ghost Market | Stolen goods, black markets, mist |
| **The Relay** | Normal physics, highest density | No faction controls it | The wild west, anything goes |

Unlocking districts means earning enough reputation — or infamy — with the controlling faction that they stop blockading the transit gates into their territory.

## Augment Progression
Movement and combat upgrades are split into two trees. You can't max both — the city's black market parts are scarce, and you have to commit to an identity:

*   **Courier tree (movement-focused)**: Grapple tether, mag-boot wall running, inertia dampeners for fall damage, speed burst, double jump implant.
*   **Sovereign tree (combat-focused)**: Subdermal armor plating, reflex stimulant (brief bullet time), target-lock overlay, threat scanner, grenade fabricator in your forearm.

A full Courier build makes you nearly impossible to catch but fragile in sustained firefights. A full Sovereign build turns you into a walking weapons platform that moves a little slower. Most players will find a hybrid that fits their style by the midgame.

## The Power Fantasy Delivery Mechanism
The thing that makes this feel like a power fantasy rather than just a hard game is **reputation broadcasting**. As your infamy grows, the city reacts to your presence in real time:

*   Low-level faction grunts hesitate before engaging, giving you a half-second window to walk through.
*   Civilians clear a path and occasionally tip you off about ambushes ahead.
*   Radio chatter between enemies references you by name with audible fear.
*   High-end faction commanders start sending personal interceptors after high-heat packages — named enemies with their own loadouts who show up across multiple runs.

**You're not becoming powerful just through stats. The city is learning to be afraid of you.**
